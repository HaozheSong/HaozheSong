Circuit Design based on a Spin–orbit Torque Device for Sensing 3D Magnetic Fields

As a member of Nanoscale Energy-Efficient Devices and Systems (NEEDS) Lab, I undertake the task to design customized circuitry for the lab's latest invention, a spin–orbit torque device for sensing 3D magnetic fields which was published on Nature Electronics in March 2021.

The Hall-Bar like device will respond to a current stimulate with a microvolt-level output. And the output is determined by both the current stimulate and the magnetic field. Therefore, a signal amplification circuit and a precise current source are required for the sensor.

Two-Stage Amplification

To read the output of the sensor to the CPU by an ADC, a two-stage amplification circuit is designed to amplify and bias the output magnify and bias the ±0.2mV output to a range of 1.5V~3.5V to meet the ADC input requirements.

The first stage reads the output from 2 pins of the sensor and magnify the output 90 times. In the power-on test shown in the pictures above, a 10mV signal was magnified to 0.9V.

The second stage makes a following 50x magnification and adds a 2.5V bias to the product, and results in an output of about 2.85V from an input of 5mV.

High-Precision Bipolar Digital Current Source

To provide a precise bipolar current, first an DAC and an amplifier constitute the voltage source with a low cost but excellent performance. Then a Howland Current Pump is introduced to covert the voltage to current linearly.

Products

The prototype failed because of a design defection which caused the OP1 to be burned out.

To verify the feasibility of all parts in the system, the whole circuity was divided to be tested individually.

The third generation succeeded but occasionally suffers from an imprecise amplification result. Currently, I am making an effort to fix it.
